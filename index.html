PURE FORM.
PURE MOTION.

A point is that which has no part. A line is breadthless length. The whole is greater than the part. Euclid — Elements, Book I

01 — Lines

The Breadthless
Length

A line is the first departure from the point — a trajectory with direction but no breadth. In motion it becomes gesture; in repetition, structure. Lines draw the boundary of every form that follows.

02 — Planes

A Surface
Stretched Thin

When a line moves along another line, it generates a plane. A surface has length and breadth but no depth — a thought extended into space, ready to receive light, shadow, and intention.

03 — Volume

The Body
Built From Shadow

A plane in motion begets volume — the third axis, where presence takes weight. Form becomes occupiable, observable from every angle, alive under light. This is where geometry becomes architecture.

Conclusion · The Axiom

FORM IS MOTION.

Every shape is a record of movement. Every movement, a shape in waiting. The axiom closes the loop — geometry, animated, returns to itself.
